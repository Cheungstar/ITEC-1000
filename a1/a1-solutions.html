9. 

In a 2 gigabyte of free space, there are 1000 megabytes per gigabyte,
so there are
2 gb x 1024 mb/gb = 2048 mb of free space.
In a megabyte, there are 1024 kilobytes,
2048 mb x 1024 kb/mb = 2097152 kb of free space.
There are 1024 bytes in a single kilobyte,
2097152 kb x 1024 b/kb = 2147483648 b of free space.
2000 characters per page, each character is assumed to take 4 bytes of memory.
2000 characters/page x 4 bytes/character = 8000 bytes/page
And so, to calculate how many pages 2 gb of free spaces can save,
2147483648 b / 8000 b/page = 268435 pages
Therefore, 268435 pages of typical text can be saved in a 2 gb of free space.

10. MPEG stands for "motion pictures experts group". MPEG-2 was the standard for the generic coding of moving pictures and is a standard that MPEG-1 improved upon. It is still relevant for the backwards compatibility newer hardware and software have with the standard as well as being widely used in DVDs, over-the-air television broadcasts, cable television, as well as direct satellite television. What it does is it is a format of compressing video, picture, and audio files in order to reduce the amount of storage space it would take up. With MPEG, data compression can reduce the size of a video file by up to 99% of the original uncompressed file. The MPEG codes saves the changes between pictures only through the use of JPEG (joint photographic experts group). 12 pictures Intra-Frames (I-Frames) that have been compressed by JPEG are saved and any other pictures between the I-Frames are saved if possible only but not in full pictures as MPEG saves only what is changed from a frame to the next frame and so on. Along with I-Frames, predicted frames and bi-directional frames (B-Frames) are also used in order to make videos play more smoothly. More information can be found here. Mpeg-2 is able to compress the highest quality video files and still establish a near 1:1 ratio between the source quality and the video produced.
